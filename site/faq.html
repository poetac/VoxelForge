FAQ

Frequently asked, honestly answered.

Scope & intent

Will an engine voxelforge generates actually fire?

Maybe. Passing every feasibility gate means the design is not obviously broken in the ways we know how to check — Bartz heat flux, Crocco stability, cavitation, rotordynamics, printability. It does not certify the engine.

Anyone planning to fire what voxelforge produces should run independent CFD, structural FEA, and a cold-flow + hot-fire campaign on a test stand. voxelforge shortens preliminary design from weeks to minutes; it does not replace verification.

Can I use this commercially?

Yes — MIT license. PicoGK is also permissively licensed. The propellant tables come from CEA, which is public-domain NASA work. Vendor LPBF process windows are referenced from public datasheets only; tighter vendor-specific bounds belong in your fork.

Why "voxelforge" and not "regen-chamber-designer"?

The project began life as RegenChamberDesigner, a regen-chamber tool. As scope grew to a full engine generator (and now a multi-pillar platform with air-breathing entries), the repo was renamed voxelforge. Sprint 0 PR-2 (2026-04-30) renamed the namespaces and assemblies; the folder rename followed 2026-05-04. Five on-disk magic strings still carry the legacy RegenChamberDesigner identifier in JSON / 3MF / STL artifacts so older saved designs keep round-tripping cleanly.

Methodology

Why simulated annealing and not gradient-based?

Two reasons. First, the search space is mixed-integer (channel count, element count, cycle topology) — gradients aren't defined. Second, the gate cascade is non-smooth: tiny parameter shifts can flip a feasibility verdict, so a smooth gradient surface doesn't exist near the boundary that matters most.

Multi-chain parallel SA with Sobol seeding shipped 2026-04-25 (ADR-017) and was promoted to default-on 2026-04-27 after the cascade benchmarks validated it: N independent chains in lockstep with elite migration, auto-scaling to ProcessorCount − 2. T1.5 progressive-fidelity pre-screen layers on top, rejecting ~60–80 % of candidates at ~10 µs each before paying the 50–200 ms thermal solve. CMA-ES on the continuous core shipped 2026-04-29; a hybrid SA+CMA-ES orchestrator and a Bayesian-optimisation outer loop are now also live.

Bartz has a ±25–50% uncertainty band. How can you optimise against it?

By treating it as a screening criterion with margin, not a target. The regen-thermal-margin gate requires predicted wall temperature stays 30 % below the Bartz worst-case. The SA isn't minimising wall temperature — it's pushing the design until any gate stops it. Bartz is one of the gates.

An SU2 RANS oracle that recalibrates the Bartz coefficient per-region for the converged candidate is in ROADMAP.md as a Tier 2 optimisation-infrastructure bet. Closed-form correlation stays the screening tool; CFD becomes the validation tool. Unscheduled.

Why no 3D CFD inside the SA loop?

Compute. A 34-D SA run with 8,000 evaluations × ~30 minutes of CFD per candidate is roughly four CPU-years. The closed-form path runs the same 8,000 evaluations in under two hours. SU2 lives outside the SA loop — applied once to the converged optimum.

How rigid is the 34-dimension search space?

Not rigid. ADR-012 documents the recipe to add a dimension: register in DesignVariableRegistry, supply bounds + log/linear scaling, write at minimum one gate that depends on it, freeze a baseline JSONL, run the cascade. The 34 are today's; new dimensions land when a physics finding opens a new SA-tunable knob.

Implementation

Why Windows-only?

PicoGK 2.2.0 ships pre-built Windows binaries with a known-good OpenVDB linkage. The Linux build path exists but isn't covered by the cascade. Headless physics in .Core runs anywhere .NET 9 runs; the Windows constraint applies only to STL generation.

Why does my test crash on dispose?

This was a known issue under PicoGK 1.7.7.5 (documented in ADR-005, now retired). Under PicoGK 2.2.0, in-process xUnit works correctly via the LibraryScope ambient helper: using var lib = new Library(vox_mm); using var libScope = LibraryScope.Set(lib); then call into builders normally. Subprocess tests are now reserved for three specific cases only: CLI-surface tests (voxelforge-eval), cross-process determinism (BenchSADeterminismTests), and cross-platform test projects.

Why pin PicoGK to 2.2.0?

Voxel-engine output is sensitive to kernel-internal numerics. Floating an unpinned PicoGK breaks reproducibility receipts on every patch release. ADR-011 fixes the version; bumps are tracked as deliberate cascade events with full baseline rebake.

What's the cascade tail and what shipped recently?

From 2026-04-22 through 2026-04-26 voxelforge ran a five-day correctness cascade: Sprint A1/A2 (the .Core / .Voxels / app split, ADR-015 + ADR-016) on day one; Sprints 30/31/32 (Criticals + 4 Majors) on day two; Sprints 33/36/34a/37a/37b/34b/38a (coolant correlations, advisory gates, turbine choke, dual-bell, aerospike Bartz, plug base drag, γ split, Rao bell table, voxelforge-eval subprocess oracle, Sobol indices, CI contracts) compressed onto days three–four; ADR-017 multi-chain SA on day four; ADR-018 feasibility-investigation audit on day five.

Phase 6 followed 2026-04-27: Sprint G′ (multi-wall hoop credit + local gas-static P), physics-integrity bundles 1+2 (closing YF-1 / ID-1 / ID-2 / ID-3), hot-fire items 5+6 (SafetyReport + BuildSheet), Tier-1 + Tier-2 audit micro-fixes, A5 LH2 viscosity, L3 Sobol slice fix, Track A (multi-chain default-on), Track B (per-station wall thickness, SA dims 28→31), plus A1 bimetallic series resistance and T1.5 progressive-fidelity SA pre-screen. 25 of 50 physics-audit findings closed; 1,613 tests passing; gate census 45 → 46.

Which canonical presets are feasible today?

Three of five, post-Phase-6: merlin (139 / 1,149 feasible @ 100-iter SA), aerospike (20 / 1,024 @ 100-iter), pintle (2,315 / 7,005 @ 500-iter — ~33 %). Pre-cascade all five returned 0 feasible. RL-10 currently sits at 0 / 15,600 — exit-station hoop on the ε=84 bell dominates yield. Track B shipped per-station wall thickness as the lever; the optimiser can now thicken the exit station while keeping the throat thin for thermal extraction. Validation run pending. pressure-fed-small at 1 kN sits in a fundamentally stretched regen regime — pressure-fed micro-thrusters typically film-cool, not regen-cool.

What is voxelforge-eval?

A subprocess oracle that wraps the headless .Core physics with a JSON-line stdin/stdout protocol. Pipe a design vector in, get back a feasibility report and headline metrics. Lets external Python / Julia / R callers drive optimisation without touching the .NET runtime. Shipped in S 38a alongside Sobol sensitivity indices.

What's a "cascade"?

A reproducibility CI job that re-runs every example, every gate scenario, and every benchmark, then diffs the resulting JSONL fingerprints against the previous baseline. A clean cascade is the merge gate. Cascade #N+1's diff against #N is the measured impact of the sprint.

How big is the codebase?

Roughly 5,700+ tests across 25+ projects spanning the 5 physics pillars (rocket · air-breathing · electric propulsion · marine · nuclear) plus an SU2 CFD-verification harness, with 40+ active ADRs (most recent: ADR-045 scheduled-workflow infrastructure). .Core holds the headless physics (no PicoGK dependency); .Voxels contains the 19 voxel builders behind IVoxelHandle; .Eval ships the voxelforge-eval subprocess oracle for Python / Julia / R consumers.

Contributing

What do you actually need help with?

Three things: validation against published hot-fire data (especially RL10 and Merlin Vacuum); the demand-driven hypergol propellant pairs (N2O4/MMH, H2O2/RP-1, N2O4/N2H4 — blocked on real CEA tables); and the SU2 oracle wiring (closed-form to CFD validation loop). All three are documented as open issues with acceptance criteria.

What kind of contributions get rejected?

Anything that breaks reproducibility receipts without a corresponding cascade rebake. Anything that adds a correlation without a citation and validity-domain guard. Anything that introduces a TODO/FIXME/HACK marker — the source tree has zero of these by policy.

Is there a community?

GitHub issues and PRs at poetac/voxelforge. No Discord, no Slack — the project leans toward asynchronous, citable, archive-friendly communication. ADRs are the canonical record.

What's the project's relationship with LEAP 71?

voxelforge uses PicoGK, an open-source voxel kernel from LEAP 71. That's the extent of the relationship. voxelforge is independent and not endorsed by, nor coordinated with, LEAP 71.
