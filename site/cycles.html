How voxelforge solves a cycle

Energy balance closes pump pressure rise against turbine drive power.

For each cycle, Core/FeedSystem/CycleSolver.cs sets up a system of equations: turbine power = pump shaft power × (1 + losses); turbine flow temperature comes from the gas-generator or preburner combustion call; pump discharge head clears injector ΔP plus regen ΔP plus chamber pressure. A Newton-Krylov iteration converges in 5–12 steps; non-convergence is itself a feasibility signal.

When the cycle won't close

Three failure modes, three gates.

  • Negative turbine inlet temperature → cycle-energy-feasible. The proposed cycle cannot extract enough enthalpy.
  • Pump head exceeds material strength → pump-head-realistic. Stage count too low.
  • Regen heat-pickup < turbine demand → expander-cycle-thermal-budget. Expander only.
