Per-pair ignition floor (S 29)

Sprint 29 replaced the universal 50 mJ floor with per-pair minimum ignition energies. Three new gates: IGNITER_MISSING, IGNITER_MODALITY_UNSUITABLE, and a tightened IGNITER_ENERGY_INSUFFICIENT.

Hypergol pairs (N2O4/MMH, H2O2/RP-1, N2O4/N2H4) are demand-driven on the public roadmap — gated on real CEA table data; ~1–2 days each once the tables arrive.

Inside the propellant tables

Tabulated, not solver-derived.

Combustion-product properties (T_c, γ, M_w, c*) are interpolated from CEA-generated tables in Core/Combustion/PropellantTables/. This is intentional — running CEA in-process would lock voxelforge into a Fortran toolchain. The price is occasional table-edge ringing at extreme O/F; gate cea-table-domain catches it.

Adding a propellant pair

ADR-pending. The recipe exists.

The recipe is documented: generate CEA tables across Pc × O/F sweep, register a PropellantPair with thermal-expansion + viscosity polynomials, register an entry in CoolantRegistry if the fuel is a coolant. Gates auto-apply once the pair is registered. The next candidates — N2O4/MMH, H2O2/RP-1, N2O4/N2H4 — are demand-driven on the roadmap; each is ~1–2 days of work once real CEA tables (MR-vs-Pc sweeps of C* / γ / Tc) arrive.
