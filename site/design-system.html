Brand & design system

An engineer's brand. No pageantry.

Five colours, two type families, a 4-px spacing grid, a small icon set, and a handful of components. The system is small on purpose — every token has a job, and the visual identity is carried by the work itself.

Logo

A monolith with a furnace mark.

A square plate stamped with the cooling-channel pattern. Forge Blue plate, Bone plate when inverted, Ignition orange spark on hover or active states. Minimum size: 16 px. Always paired with the wordmark in Inter Tight 600.

Colour

Five core, three families.

Forge Blue is the structural voice — heading accents, link colour, infographics. Ignition is the spark — used sparingly, signals action and active state. Graphite is the body. Bone is the page. Steel is the soft text.

Typography

Three families, a clear hierarchy.

Fraunces for editorial display moments — hero numbers and pull-quotes only. Inter Tight for everything else. JetBrains Mono for code, formulas, gate IDs, and metric labels.

Spacing & radii

4-px grid. Two radii.

Spacing is a multiplicative scale on a 4 px base. Use named tokens (--s-3, --s-5) — never literal values. Radii: 6 px for inline elements, 12 px for cards, full pill for tags and buttons.

Illustration system

Schematic, not photographic.

Hero imagery uses the actual PicoGK renders (chamber-side, chamber-cutaway, chamber-throat). Inline diagrams are SVG: 1.5–2 px strokes, fills only for emphasis, Forge Blue for fluid paths, Ignition for hot gas, Graphite for shafts, Steel for inert lines. No gradients except as low-opacity backgrounds for technical canvases.
